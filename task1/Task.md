# Task instructions

## Introduction to the task

Your task is to create a comprehensive report that details the key responsibilities of an actuarial analyst, explains the basics of marine insurance with a focus on Marine Third-Party Liability, and outlines an experience-based pricing analysis for Marine Liability insurance. This report will serve as a foundation for the pricing model we will develop later in the project.

## Instructions

**Identify and describe the key responsibilities of an actuarial analyst**

- Research the daily tasks and overall responsibilities of an actuarial analyst in the general insurance sector.
- Focus on areas such as risk assessment, pricing, reserving, financial reporting, and regulatory compliance.
- Write a detailed section in your report describing these responsibilities, emphasizing their importance in maintaining the financial health of an insurance company.

**Gain a basic understanding of marine insurance, with a particular focus on Marine Third-Party Liability**

- Study the basics of marine insurance, focusing on common perils and different types of coverages.
- Pay special attention to Marine Third-Party Liability, which includes coverage for legal liabilities to third parties such as bodily injury, property damage, pollution liability, and wreck removal.
- Write a section in your report summarizing your understanding of marine insurance, with clear definitions and examples of Marine Third-Party Liability.

**Create a basic outline for pricing analyses for Marine Liability business**

-Develop an outline for conducting a pricing analysis using experience-based rating.
-Include key steps such as data collection, data cleaning and preparation, risk assessment, rate calculation, validation and testing, and documentation.
=Provide explanations for each step in your outline, highlighting the importance of adjusting historical data for inflation and other factors to ensure accuracy.
- Include this outline in your report.

**Deliverable**
Submit a report that is concise, accurate, and demonstrates your understanding of the topics.

- Format: PDF or Word document
